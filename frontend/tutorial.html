Understanding Parameters

C/N Ratio

Optimal: 25-30

The carbon-to-nitrogen ratio is crucial for microbial activity. Too high = slow decomposition, too low = odor problems.

Moisture Level

Optimal: 50-60%

Moisture is essential for microbial activity. Too dry = slow process, too wet = anaerobic conditions.

Temperature

Optimal: 55-65°C

High temperatures indicate active decomposition and help kill pathogens and weed seeds.

Aeration

Optimal: 3-5 times/week

Oxygen is needed for aerobic decomposition. Regular turning prevents anaerobic conditions.

Odor Level

Optimal: 1-2 (earthy smell)

Strong odors indicate anaerobic conditions or imbalanced nutrients. Good compost smells earthy.

NPK Values

Higher is better

Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium are essential plant nutrients. Higher values indicate richer compost.

Efficiency Score Calculation

The efficiency score (0-100) is calculated based on how well your composting parameters align with optimal ranges:

  • C/N Ratio (20 points): Best score for 25-30 ratio
  • Moisture Level (20 points): Best score for 50-60%
  • Temperature (20 points): Best score for 55-65°C
  • Aeration (15 points): Best score for 3-5 times per week
  • Odor Level (10 points): Best score for levels 1-2
  • Decomposition Time (15 points): Bonus points for faster decomposition

Best Practices Tips

Material Balance

Mix 3 parts brown materials (carbon) with 1 part green materials (nitrogen) for optimal C/N ratio.

Monitor Regularly

Check temperature, moisture, and odor weekly. Adjust aeration and materials as needed.

Size Matters

Chop materials into smaller pieces (1-2 inches) to speed up decomposition process.

Temperature Phases

Expect mesophilic (20-40°C) → thermophilic (40-70°C) → cooling → maturation phases.

Troubleshooting

Strong Ammonia Smell

Cause: Too much nitrogen (low C/N ratio)

Solution: Add brown materials (leaves, paper, cardboard)

Low Temperature

Cause: Too dry, too small pile, or wrong C/N ratio

Solution: Add water, increase pile size, adjust materials

Pests or Flies

Cause: Food scraps on surface or too wet

Solution: Bury food scraps, reduce moisture, turn more frequently

Slow Decomposition

Cause: High C/N ratio, too dry, lack of aeration

Solution: Add green materials, increase moisture, turn pile regularly
